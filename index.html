ULRS3032 Entreprenuership and Innovation

Engages students in real entrepreneurship experience by introducing the fundamentals of business ideation, innovation thinking, and venture creation. Challenges students to identify real-world problems, develop viable product or service solutions, and pitch their ideas — bridging the gap between technical skills and business acumen essential for a well-rounded data engineering professional.

Project-
Sparkling Dumpling Project

Organised a charity fundraising initiative centred around a dumpling business, channelling proceeds toward a meaningful cause — visiting a women's shelter to engage residents through activities that uplift, encourage, and bring joy. A project that proved entrepreneurship is not just about profit, but about creating real impact in the community.

My Reflection — Entreprenuership and Innovation

What I gained: This course gave me my first real taste of entrepreneurship — not as a theory in a textbook, but as a lived experience with real planning, real people, and a real cause. Organising the Sparkling Dumpling fundraiser taught me how much thought goes into even a small business: sourcing, pricing, marketing, logistics, and managing a team all at once. It was challenging in the best possible way.

Most meaningful moment: Visiting the women's shelter and seeing the residents light up during our activities was something no grade or certificate could replicate. It reminded me that the best innovations are the ones built with empathy — designed not just to generate value, but to genuinely improve someone's day or life. That visit will stay with me for a long time.

How it changed me: I came into this course thinking entrepreneurship was purely about business and profit. I left understanding that the most powerful ventures are the ones with a clear social purpose behind them. As a future data engineer, I now think more consciously about who my work serves and what impact it creates beyond the pipeline.

Suggested improvements: I would love more structured mentoring sessions with real entrepreneurs or social enterprise founders — hearing how they balance profit and purpose would add tremendous depth to this course.
